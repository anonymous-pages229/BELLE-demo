01 · Abstract

A distribution, not a point.

PRIORNormal-Inverse-Gamma
PARAMSμ, ν, α, β
CORPORAMultiVox · LibriSpeech+6TTS
REGIMEOne-to-many AR

Text-to-Speech (TTS) is inherently a one-to-many mapping characterized by intrinsic uncertainty, yet current paradigms often oversimplify it into a deterministic regression task. While continuous-valued autoregressive (AR) models have recently emerged as a promising alternative to discrete codec-based approaches, they typically rely on a fixed-variance prior, fundamentally constraining generation to a static point estimate that ignores the dynamic variability of natural speech.

To bridge this gap, we propose BELLE (Bayesian evidential learning with language modelling), a framework that shifts from deterministic prediction to principled Bayesian inference without increasing model parameters or inference latency. By modelling the acoustic target as a Normal-Inverse-Gamma distribution, BELLE captures data-dependent aleatoric uncertainty.

Variance estimation under this prior, however, requires multiple speech realizations per text, motivating a one-to-many training strategy that treats parallel speech as a statistical support set rather than imitation targets. Two parallel-speech corpora are constructed for this purpose — MultiVox, assembled from real human recordings, and LibriSpeech+6TTS, synthesized by six pre-trained TTS teachers — and BELLE learns within-prompt diversity from either source.

Across both corpora, BELLE consistently outperforms deterministic baselines on quality, speaker similarity, and within-prompt diversity, and the framework extends naturally to streaming generation.
